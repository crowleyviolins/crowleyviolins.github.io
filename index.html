  2. About Me

    Sound is my craft and passion. As a violin maker and musician I strive to create instruments with the utmost dedication to this passion. Carefully selecting each piece of wood and meticulously working through every detail, consciously making choices, knowing that every decision impacts the tone, color and projection of sound. In addition to sounding great under the ear and projecting throughout the hall, each violin, viola, and cello also needs to have a personality that allows a musician to connect with something that’s alive. The product of my work not only has to look and sound good, but to feel like an extension of a player’s hands. It has to be responsive to the moment and go from carrying above an orchestra to balancing into a string quartet. All the while keeping a balance and range of tonal colors and its fullness of sound.

    My inspiration comes from the masters of the 17th century and I have extensively studied the work of Stradarvari and Guarneri del Gesu. Informed by their iconic work, I also keep in mind the demands a modern player places on their instruments. So I am engaged in constant experimentation and evolution, seeking a balance of timeless designs with modern sensibilities. I seek to always craft the best sounding instrument possible, while achieving a beautiful and classic aesthetic.

    Today, modern violin making is experiencing a renaissance and truly offers a fantastic alternative to antique instruments and the astronomical prices they command. The best modern instruments are not only a great bargain, but also have real advantages over their antique counterparts. I welcome inquiries to discuss creating your ideal instrument.

    Biography

    Paul started playing music at the tender age of eight and made his first guitar in 1993. In college, he majored in music and studied bass with Drew Gress. He then played bass professionally for several years and has also worked as a recording and live sound engineer. In 2002, Paul graduated from the North Bennett Street School's renowned violin making program. Upon graduation, he worked with Michele Ashley and Marco Coppiardi, learning violin making in the Italian tradition. Paul regularly attends the Oberlin College violin-making workshop where some of the finest violin makers in the world come together for two weeks each summer to collaborate and share new ideas. In 2004, one of his violins won a silver medal at the VSA international violin making competition. He was elected to the American Federation of Violin and Bow Makers in 2010 and is a member of the Violin Society of America. To date, he has made approximately 100 violins, violas and cellos.
